Frequently Asked Questions

A lawyer may be able to help you obtain compensation after a car accident. To do this, they, or their investigators, may use various techniques (such as accident reconstruction) to assign liability. A car accident lawyer may also obtain traffic surveillance or business establishment videos that capture the crash in real-time; or review police reports to look for mistakes that could have an undesirable effect on the outcome of your case.

Wilshire Law Firm may negotiate your car accident settlement by identifying liable parties, determining fault, and collecting evidence that may prove your claim. Our experienced team knows that negotiation is an important part of the lawsuit process and will not accept a lowball figure from an insurer. It is vital to work with an attorney because accepting a settlement that does not cover damages can be financially devastating.

Remember, once you accept an insurance company’s offer and sign a waiver that you no longer hold them liable, you are on your own, even if more medical care is necessary.

In most cases, the statute of limitations for filing a personal injury claim is two years from the accident date. However, there are complicating factors that can either shorten or lengthen the time that a victim must file a claim following an accident. To protect your claim, you should retain the services of a local personal injury attorney so that you can ensure that you receive the maximum compensation you deserve.
